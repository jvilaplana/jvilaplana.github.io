Problem #6

The planner has a short-term planning algorithm with 3 multilevel queues:

  • Queue 1 plans priority real time processes and uses a FCFS algorithm. This is the most prioritary queue.
  • Queue 2 plans real time processes and uses a Round-Robin algorithm with a 1 cycle quantum. This is the 2nd priority queue.
  • Queue 3 plans user processes and uses a Round-Robin algorithm with a 3 cycles quantum. This is the 3rd priority queue.
  • Queue 4 plans batch processes and uses a SRT (Short Remaining Time) algorithm. This queue is the lowest priority queue.

The planning between queues follows a prioritary algorithm, such that a process from a lower priority queue can be only executed if there is not any prepared process in the more priority queues. Moreover, higher priority processes take over the CPU immediately when they can be executed.

To break ties between processes of the same queue, its priority will be used (1 is highest, 6 is lowest).
